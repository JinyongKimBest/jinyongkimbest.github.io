Battery Research

We tackle the three key challenges in EV industry by developing various multi-physics simulation tools. We consider various physical phenomena simulataneously.
We lead the battery safety simulation research. Prof. Kim leading REAL developed the very first tool for battery fire simulation, which was later implemented in Converge CFD.
We seek to develop robust numerical algorithms so that our simulation tools are very practical for industrial uses.
We plan to develop simulation tools for Li-ion batteries with composite anodes and solid-state batteries.

Fuel Cell Research

One of the main challenges in FCEV industry is the high cost. Improving operating current density of fuel cell electrodes can reduce the manufacture cost by increasing the power density of fuel cell stacks. To increase the operating current density of PEMFCs, it is essential to optimize electodes for facile oxygen transport and effectively manage the liquid water generated during operation.
Prof. Kim leading REAL developed a simulation tool considering two-phase Forchheimer's inertial effect. It is shown that the two-phase Forchheimer's inertial effect helps liquid water removal in cathode flow channels and electrodes, which leads improved oxygen transport during high-current density operation.
